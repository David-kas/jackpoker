Weekly review without theater

Pick a fixed calendar slot, even thirty minutes, and label hands with one actionable tag: “fold earlier,” “size up for protection,” “three-bet frequency drift.” The goal is pattern recognition, not a dramatic montage. If a week produces zero tags, you are either not saving hands or not being honest about mistakes—both are fixable process bugs.

When you import solver outputs, treat them as sensitivity checks, not verdicts. Ask what assumption changed the recommendation—range width, bet size step, or stack depth—then test that single variable in play. Spreading five new concepts at once makes feedback loops noisy and confidence brittle.
